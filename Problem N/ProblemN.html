Problem N - Janny and Jaffy's Wedding

Description

Not only is Janny enrolling at UMW, but Janny and Jaffy are getting married. When Janny received the ring you printed in Problem H, she and Jaffy decided to get married. Giraffes tend to marry early. Jeography is Jaffy's best man, and Jeography has flown to Fredericksburg to help Jaffy with wedding details. Jeography asks Jaffy if they will have sparkling grape juice at the wedding as it is a favourite beverage of giraffes. Giraffes are a bit quirky when it comes to drinking sparkling grape juice. Giraffes have unique taste buds when it comes to drinking sparkling grape juice. Each giraffe only likes to drink sparkling grape juice when it's at a certain temperature as any other temperature doesn't taste quite right. Each wedding guest has their own drinkable sparkling grape juice temperature. Since Jaffy and Janny want their wedding to be perfect, they will only serve sparkling grape juice at temperatures everyone will enjoy. To make sure everyone is satisfied, he makes sure each giraffe has their own bottle at the temperature they want. The fridge at the wedding has an internal temperature of 40 degrees Fahrenheit and Jaffy knows that every minute outside the fridge the grape juice will be 0.5 degrees warmer. Jaffy wants a list of the times he should pull his friend's grape juice bottles out ordered by how far from the wedding he should pull out the bottles.

Input


  • The first N lines define wedding guests and their desired temperature of sparkling grape juice. Each line contains two values. The name of the guest is a string without spaces. Following the name is a space. Following the space is a non-negative integer, which is the guest's desired temperature. each contain one integer representing the temperature desired by the guest and the string of the guest's name.
  • The line after the guest's request contains a single period.
  • The next line is an integer being the temperature outside on the wedding day

    • Output

    The output is the time, an integer for the hours and an integer for the minutes when the bottle should be pulled out for each guest that day of the wedding. If the guest wants a temperature we are unable to obtain, print out their name: and say " unable to get the right temperature." The output should be printed out in descending order by time away from the wedding followed by those that requested invalid temperatures. Sample Input 

    Jaffy 70
Janny 40 
Jeography 10 
Gingersnap 80
.
70

    Sample Output 

    Jaffy: remove bottle from fridge 1 hours and 0 minutes before the wedding.
Janny: remove bottle from fridge at the time of the wedding.
Jeography: unable to get the right temperature.
Gingersnap: unable to get the right temperature.
